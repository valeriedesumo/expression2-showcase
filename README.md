## Background
In 2004, Valve Corporation released the renowned video games Half-Life 2 and Counter-Strike: Source, which ran on a newly designed video game engine, known as [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Source_(game_engine)). This engine would later be used to power Team Fortress 2, Dota 2, and Portal. It was designed to be [mod-friendly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mod_(video_gaming)), and one of the popular mods was known as [Garry's Mod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garry%27s_Mod), or Gmod.

Gmod was developed and expanded over the years, eventually becoming its own individual game. Community members and players began developing game modes and mods for Gmod itself, one of which is called [Wiremod](http://wiki.wiremod.com/wiki/Main_Page) ([GitHub](https://github.com/wiremod/wire)). Wiremod adds items such as sensors, inputs, logic gates, and programmable chips, to Gmod. This allows players to programmatically control outputs such as text display, rocket thrusters, explosives, doors, turrets, wheels, and more.

## Expression 2
One of Wiremod's tools is the [Expression 2 chip](http://wiki.wiremod.com/wiki/Expression_2), which is an in-game programmable device which can read data about the current gameplay or receive data from inputs, and send outputs to other Wiremod items, create models ("holograms"), and even apply a physical force onto an entity.

In this repository are samples of the code I've written for this chip. Feel free to contact me if you wish to see these in-game.
